\section{Conclusion and Future Work}

\begin{frame}{Conclusion and Future Work}
  \begin{block}{Summary}
    
    \begin{itemize}[<+->]
      \item FedITN generates \emph{diverse}, \emph{constraint-compliant} network topologies using attack scenarios and a library of \emph{sub-topologies}.
      \item Preliminary results show a capacity to generate a \emph{wide range of variations}, but face scalability issues due to the number of similar topologies generated. 
      \item Opens the way to \emph{various research directions}: \textit{real} heterogeneity, collection placement, robustness to drift, topology-dependent models...
    \end{itemize} 
  \end{block}

  \pause
  \begin{block}{Future Work}
    \begin{itemize}[<+->]
      \item \emph{Data generation}! Fill the topologies and attack scenarios; handle traffic generation and labeling following the recent works on data quality.
      \item Design appropriate testing strategies (testing sets).
      \item Improve constraint definition; optimize the selection and composition algorithms.
      \item Distance ourselves from the platform dependency generalize the approach.
    \end{itemize}
  \end{block}
\end{frame}
